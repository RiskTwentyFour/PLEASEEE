Carbonara

Carbonara comes from Italy, specifically the region of Lazio, with Rome being the most famous. The name “carbonara” is believed to come from “carbonaro”, which means charcoal burner in Italian. Some say it was a dish popular among Italian coal workers, while others think it refers to the black pepper that looks like coal dust.


Why carbonara is the best food: Carbonara is simple yet incredibly flavorful. The creamy sauce, made from eggs and cheese, perfectly hugs the pasta, while the salty pancetta or guanciale adds a savory, smoky touch. Every bite feels rich and comforting, but it never feels too heavy. It’s a dish that combines warmth, taste, and comfort all in one, making it a timeless favorite for many.

Yakult

Yakult comes from Japan and was created in 1935 by Dr. Minoru Shirota. The name “Yakult” comes from the Esperanto word “Jahurto”, which means yogurt. It is a fermented drink made with probiotic bacteria that is good for digestion and gut health.


Why Yakult is the best drink: Yakult is small but powerful. It is sweet, tangy, and refreshing, and it makes your stomach feel good because of the probiotics. It is a drink that gives energy, boosts health, and makes you feel light and happy. It’s simple, convenient, and full of care in every small bottle, making it a drink that many people love every day.

Calamares

Calamares comes from Spain, and the word “calamares” means squid in Spanish. It is usually sliced into rings, coated in batter, and deep-fried until crispy. It became popular in the Philippines and many other countries as a tasty snack or street food.

Mang Inasal

Inasal comes from the Philippines, specifically Bacolod in the Visayas region. It is a style of grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of calamansi, vinegar, garlic, and annatto, giving it a flavorful and slightly smoky taste. The word “inasal” means “to roast or grill” in the local language.


Why Inasal is the best street food: Inasal is juicy, tender, and full of flavor. The grilled aroma alone makes your mouth water, and it is perfect to enjoy with rice, dipping sauces, or even a side of pickled vegetables. It is a dish that feels hearty, comforting, and truly satisfying simple food that always leaves you happy.
